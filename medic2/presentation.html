MONITORING OF DRUG SAFETY

Pharmacovigilance

Pharmacological science and practical activities  are relating to the collection, detection, assessment, monitoring, and prevention of adverse effects  with drugs

Medical products that are in circulation in the Russian Federation shall be monitored of the security in order to detect the possible negative consequences, warning patients and their protection against the use of such drugs.

(Federal Law No. 61-FZ of 12.04.2010 "On the circulation of medicinal products" article 64).

REGULATORY AND LEGAL FRAMEWORK OF PHARMACOVIGILANCE

  • Federal Law No. 61-FZ of 12.04.2010 "On the circulation of medicinal products“
  • The letter of Federal Service for Surveillance in Healthcare 14.06.2017 N-1420/17 "On the new legislative requirements for pharmacovigilance“;
  • Order of Federal Service for Surveillance in Healthcare No. 1071 of 15.02.2017 "On approval of the procedure for the implementation of pharmacovigilance”
  • Guideline on good pharmacovigilance practices (GVP) of the Eurasian Economic Union, approved by the Council of the Eurasian Economic Commission 03.11.2016 2016 №87
  • Good Clinical Practice (GCP), approved by the Council of ECE 03.11.2016 2016 No. 79
  • Russian Ministry of Health order from 07.09.2015 N 5539 "On approval of a sample implementation of quality control of drugs for medical use“
  • Ministry of Health and Social Development order of the Russian Federation of 26.08.2010 N 758n "On Approving the Procedure for Suspending the Use of a Medicinal Product for Medical Use« (Russian Ministry of Justice registered 31.08.2010 N 18325)
  • Russian Ministry of Health order of 26.08.2010 N 749n "On approval of the form of the documents containing the results of safety monitoring of the medicinal products for medical use with the aim of confirming its state of the  registration"
  • Russian Ministry of Health order of 07.09.2016 №682n "On approval of the form of the documents containing the results of monitoring the efficacy and safety of the medicinal product for the medical use conducted by the holder or by the owner of the registration certificate of a medical or deprived legal entity"
  • National standard "Appropriate Clinical Practice. GOST R 52379-2005 ", approved by the order of Russian technical regulation system from 27.09.2005 N 232-s.

REGULATORY MEASURES IN THE IDENTIFICATION OF SAFETY PROBLEMS OF DRUGS

  • Cancelling of the registration (Art. 32 of the Federal Law №61)
  • Suspension of medical products circulation (Art. 65 of the Federal Law №61)
  • Administrative punishment (Art. 68 of the Federal Law №61)
  • Criminal Code of the Russian Federation, Article 238 “ Production, storage, transportation or sale of goods and products, perfomance of the works or services, not meeting safety requirements)
  • Changes to the medical instruction (order of Federal Service for Surveillance in Healthcare №1071 from 15.02.2017)
  • Changes to the Protocol of medical trials (Art. 40 of the Federal Law №61)
  • Suspension, delay of medical trials (Art. 40 of the Federal Law №61)
  • Request for Risk Management Plan (RMP)
  • Additional monitoring drug safety (ЕАЭС GVP)

ADDITIONAL REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS OF OF THE EURASIAN ECONOMIC UNION

  1. Harmonisation of the pharmacovigilance system Russian Federation with GVP, GCP of the Eurasian Economic Union and EU GVP;
  2. The obligation to provide the Master file by request from Federal Service for Surveillance in Healthcare;
  3. The requirement for the presence of the Pharmacovigilance Officer on Eurasian Economic Union;
  4. Requirements for screening local scientific literature;
  5. Possible laboratory control of drugs if we got adverse reactions in sample test;
  6. Requirements to training local staff on pharmacovigilance;
  7. Possible to request RMP of registered drugs before implementation of risk management measures;
  8. Requirements to create risk management programs considering specific Federal Service for Surveillance in Healthcare
  9. Impossibility of copying European PSUR
  10. Informing regulators by holders of registration certificates about important changes to the pharmacovigilance system;
  11. Planned and extraordinary inspection pharmacovigilance system;
  12. The appointment of post-marketing clinical trials;
  13. Requirements for informing doctors and consumers about the new data safety of drugs
INDEPENDENT CREATION OF THE PHARMACOVIGILANCE SYSTEM
  • Prepare a master file of the pharmacovigilance system;
  • Appoint the Pharmacovigilance Officer and notify the Federal Service for Surveillance in Healthcare.
  • Educate Pharmacovigilance Officer and all employees;
  • Create standard operating procedures (SOP) for the main pharmacovigilance processes based on the company's quality management system
  • Ensure the implementation of the SOP, leaving a documentary trace
  • To provide a continuous and qualitative analysis of the balance of benefits and risks of drugs
  • Organize the work of an electronic database of  adverse drug reaction
  • Organize the collection, analysis and evaluation of adverse drug reaction's data
  • Screening scientific literature
  • Prepare periodic security reports in a new format (PRBER)
  • Self-detection and Safety Signal Processing of medicines
  • Independent development of the risk management strategy and risk management plans of the medicines
  • Ensure full and timely submission of responses to regulatory requests
  • Providing regular audits (self-testing)
  • Preparation for inspections of regulatory bodies and inspections commissions (external audits - since 2017)
