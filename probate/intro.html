Overview of the Georgia Probate Process

One way we simplify the process of settling an estate is by dividing it into three phases.

Phase 1: Appointment

The goal of Phase 1 is to have someone appointed as the Administrator of the estate by the Probate Court.

Continue

Phase 2: Appointment

key things that must be done in Phase 2 are to assess assets and creditors of the estate, determining what will be done with each asset and creditor, and identifying what property is likely to be distributed to heirs or beneficiaries.

Phase 3: Appointment

The main goals of this phase are to successfully distribute all remaining assets of the estate to the heirs or beneficiaries
