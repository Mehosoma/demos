Phase 2: Administration

We call the second phase the administration phase. This is the longest of the three phases. Some of the key things that must be done in Phase 2 are to assess assets and creditors of the estate, determining what will be done with each asset and creditor, and identifying what property is likely to be distributed to heirs or beneficiaries.

Phase 3: Distributions & Discharge

The third Phase is called Distribution & Discharge. The main goals of this phase are to successfully distribute all remaining assets of the estate to the heirs or beneficiaries, and then to close the estate in such a way that the executor or administrator is protected from liability for anything that happened while the estate was open. The liability protection is important because that is what gives the executor or administrator the peace of mind to know that the estate is finished, and nothing can come back to haunt them later.
